# Pressed in the Pages

Old Mr. Quill, the chandler at Saltmark's south quay, kept an almanac for the city's tide times and saint's days — *Quill's Common Almanac, 1798 Edition*. A copy of the PDF wound up on our desk yesterday, with a note: *"Read the typesetting, not the words."*

The pages render normally in any reader. The text is intact. But Quill — secretly a clever man — typeset certain glyphs in a near-identical custom font, indistinguishable to the human eye but distinct to anyone who walks the PDF's content stream. Those glyphs spell out a longer message, more than 60 characters. Don't try to read it by hand.

The Treasure is the message hidden in the typography.
