Sailing the lesser charts.

Welcome aboard. This is the public diary of Captain Jolly Wexler, master of the twelve-fathom sloop Hesper, kept since the second tide of last spring. I publish posts when I make port and have hands free for ink. The full diary is over on the blog.

If you only want the daily tide observations I take at every anchorage, those go to a separate feed at /blog/jolly-wexler/tide.rss — a habit I picked up so that the chart-wardens at the regional weather archive could verify my readings against theirs. (More on that habit in this post.)

About me. Captain "Jolly" Wexler. Born in the village of Saltmark, raised between the tide pools and the chandlery. Five-time crewman of the Reefborn Five, three-time skipper. I sail the lesser-known harbours of the Crescent because the famous ones are full of lawyers and the lesser ones are full of weather.
