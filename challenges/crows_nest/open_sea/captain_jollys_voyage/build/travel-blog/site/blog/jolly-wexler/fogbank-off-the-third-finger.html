Fogbank off the Third Finger

2026-04-03 · by Jolly Wexler

I learnt to sail in fog before I learnt to sail in clear weather, which I credit for having survived as long as I have. Yesterday I came into a wall of it just east of the Third Finger reef — you couldn't see the headland, you couldn't hear the bell buoy, you couldn't see your own hands on the tiller. I dropped the storm jib, killed the way on the boat, and waited.

Two hours later it lifted, the way they always do, and there was the reef where I expected it — about three cables north and slightly to port. I had drifted exactly as the chart said I would. The dead-reckoning works.

Tide reading at the time was 1.8m, falling, recorded for the journal. The wardens at the regional archive can have it if they want it; I doubt anyone else cares about a fogbank tide off an unnamed reef.
