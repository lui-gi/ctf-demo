Three days becalmed

2026-04-12 · by Jolly Wexler

Three days of nothing. The wind died on the morning of the tenth and hasn't come back. I am drifting at the rate of whatever the current decides, which according to my own dead reckoning is about a knot and a half east-southeast. At this rate I will arrive somewhere by accident around the middle of next week.

The tide journal has been very dull reading. Yesterday's entry said "tide: 2.1m, slack" and the day before said "tide: 2.0m, slack". There is a pleasure in dull reports the way there is a pleasure in flat seas: it means you have time to think.

I have been thinking about what I'll do when I make the final harbour of this run. The truthful answer is that I do not know. There is a cliff path I want to walk. There may or may not be a tavern. We'll find out.
