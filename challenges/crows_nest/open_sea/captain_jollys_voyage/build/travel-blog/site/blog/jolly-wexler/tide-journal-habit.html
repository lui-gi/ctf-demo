A note on the tide journal habit

2026-03-22 · by Jolly Wexler

Every day at the top of an hour — usually 19:00 UTC if I'm anchored, sometimes the next round hour if I'm under way — I take a tide reading wherever I happen to be: the height in metres, the direction (rising, slack, or falling), and the timestamp. I post the reading to a small RSS feed at /blog/jolly-wexler/tide.rss so that the chart-wardens at the regional weather archive can compare my numbers against theirs.

The archive itself is hosted alongside this blog at /charts/tides/ — it's a public catalogue of every harbour in the Crescent that anyone has bothered to take a tide reading at. Each harbour gets its own CSV with a month of hourly observations. If my reading and theirs disagree by more than a hand's breadth on a calm day, somebody is wrong, and it's usually me.

The point of all this, in case it isn't obvious: a tide reading at a known timestamp is enough information to identify a harbour, because no two harbours in the Crescent share a tide signature exactly. So the journal also serves as a rough position log without me having to admit where I am directly. (Old habits. I prefer obliquity.)

If you want the feed, it's /blog/jolly-wexler/tide.rss. If you want the archive, it's /charts/tides/. Both are here as long as I keep paying the hosting bill, which I always have, even when I haven't.
