Chartwork and other small comforts

2026-03-09 · by Jolly Wexler

Spent the morning at the chart table updating soundings from last winter's wardens' bulletin. Half the depths in this corner of the Crescent were last surveyed in my grandfather's day, and the bottom shifts faster than the surveyors do. I keep a personal correction sheet for every chart I rely on.

The afternoon was for inventory: spare cordage, spare canvas, a small sail repair, and four jars of brined limes that are starting to look as old as I feel. Then a shore-side meal in a tavern whose name I have already forgotten, and back to the boat.

One of these days I'll write up the tide journal habit properly — how to keep the readings, why I bother posting them, what the wardens at the archive do with them. That's a post for another evening.
